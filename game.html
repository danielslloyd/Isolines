A fractal ravine shelters your keep — sheer cliffs on every side, and one narrow mouth to the south.

How to play
  • Camera: WASD pans, Q/E or middle-drag rotates, wheel zooms.
  • Walls: click to place points, Enter or double-click to build, right-click to undo, Esc to cancel. Walls snap onto ridges and join existing walls seamlessly.
  • Cliffs count as walls: sealing the ravine mouth protects everything inside the canyon.
  • High ground is armor: walls on hills take less damage, towers on cliffs and ramparts shoot farther.
  • Enclose houses to protect them — ⚠ marks exposed homes. Protected civilians pay taxes each wave.
  • Add a gate to a walled enclosure for +25% trade income; rams will head for it.
  • Attackers besiege your weakest, lowest stretch of wall. Watch the red circles.
  • Each level the settlement grows outward — down the valley, up the ramps — so keep extending and upgrading: palisade to stone, stone to fortified citadel walls.
  • Eras escalate: Bronze Age raids → Iron Age rams → medieval catapults, sappers, siege towers, trebuchets.
  • Space starts the next wave. You can build during a siege if enemies aren't standing on the site.
